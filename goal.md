# Research Goal: Albert Einstein

## Subject
Albert Einstein — theoretical physicist, Nobel laureate, and one of the most influential scientists in history.

## Anchors
- Born: March 14, 1879, Ulm, Germany
- Died: April 18, 1955, Princeton, New Jersey
- Nobel Prize in Physics, 1921 (photoelectric effect)
- Key institutions: Swiss Patent Office, University of Zurich, Prussian Academy of Sciences, Institute for Advanced Study (Princeton)
- Key papers: Special Relativity (1905), General Relativity (1915), Photoelectric Effect (1905), Brownian Motion (1905), E=mc² (1905)

## What I Want to Understand
- Scientific contributions and their impact on modern physics
- Education, early life, and intellectual development
- Views on philosophy, politics, and social justice
- Later years at Princeton and legacy
- Key relationships and collaborations (Grossmann, Besso, Bohr, Born, Hilbert)

## Completion Criteria
- All accepted task Issues closed with HIGH confidence
- Zero unresolved contradictions between sections
- Every finding backed by 2+ sources, at least 1 Tier 1 (primary/academic)
- Coverage of all five areas listed above

## Out of Scope
- Detailed mathematical derivations of theories
- Personal romantic relationships beyond what's publicly documented
- Conspiracy theories or pseudoscientific claims about Einstein
